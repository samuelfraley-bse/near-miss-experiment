Information Sheet

Title of the project: Near-Miss and Skill Framed Games

Institution: Universitat Pompeu Fabra / Barcelona School of Economics

Principal Investigators: Samuel Fraley (samuel.fraley@bse.eu), Corneel Moons (corneel.moons@bse.eu), Tizian Schenk (tizian.schenk@bse.eu)

Contact information: If you have doubts, you may contact the principal investigators.

If you have doubts, complaints, or questions about this study or your rights as a research participant, you may contact UPF’s Institutional Committee for the Ethical Review of Projects (CIREP) by phone (+34 93 542 21 86) or by email (secretaria.cirep@upf.edu). CIREP is not part of the research team and will treat any information you send confidentially.

Funding body: No funding.

Objectives of the project: The goal of this study is to evaluate how the near-miss effect impacts participants’ persistence in skill-framed games, compared to traditionally researched luck-framed games.

Methodology and participation: This study is conducted online and takes approximately 5 minutes to complete in a single session. Participants complete a short game task of 5 rounds and then answer a brief questionnaire about their experience, perceptions, and motivation. We collect anonymous responses on game performance (for example, round outcomes), survey answers, and basic demographics (age, gender, and course membership) to analyze how different types of feedback influence perception and behavioral intentions. Participation takes place remotely on a web interface using the participant’s own device.

Inclusion criteria: None.

Voluntary participation: Your participation in this study is voluntary and you may withdraw at any time without having to justify your decision.

Risks and benefits: Participating in this study does not entail risks greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life. We cannot and do not guarantee that you will receive any direct personal benefits from this study.

Compensation: None.

Data protection: In order to protect your privacy, data are stored using a participant code rather than your name. Data are accessible only to authorized research team members. If results are published, only anonymous data will be shared.

The data collected during the experiment cannot be used to identify you. Thus, once the experiment concludes, we will not be able to delete the data you generated, should you ask for it. Anonymized data may be hosted or published in a public repository.

GDPR Information (UPF): Data controller: Universitat Pompeu Fabra, C. de la Merce, 12, 08002 Barcelona, Tel. +34 93 542 20 00. Data Protection Officer contact: dpd@upf.edu.

Purpose of processing: Carrying out the above-mentioned research project. Personal data will be kept during project execution and for two more years after completion for scientific validation. If a payment is involved, necessary data will be kept in accordance with applicable regulation.

Legal basis: Your consent, which can be withdrawn at any time.

Recipients: Your personal data will only be processed by Universitat Pompeu Fabra and will not be transferred to third parties without your consent, except as otherwise provided by law. Data may be anonymized and published in an open science repository.

Your rights: You may access your data; request rectification, deletion, portability (where applicable); object to processing; and request limitation of processing through www.upf.edu/web/proteccio-dades/drets. You may contact dpd@upf.edu and, if needed, file a complaint with the Catalan Data Protection Authority (apdcat.gencat.cat).
